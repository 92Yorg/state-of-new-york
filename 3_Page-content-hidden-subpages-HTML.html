New York City has always been at the forefront of culture, politics, and arts. As a global epicenter, New York City’s vibrant arts and culture scene attracts and keeps millions of people every year. After surviving a global pandemic, New York City faces looming budget deficits, concerns about quality of life and a rapidly shifting economy. So we ask the question: what’s next for New York?

The State of New York, hosted by the Newmark Civic Life Series at The 92nd Street Y, New York, will convene conversations around the most pressing issues facing our City right now and how those challenges intersect and impact the Arts. The Series examines how issues like the housing crisis, a flailing education system, public safety issues, and philanthropic giving shape our city and its future with New York’s leading decision-makers.
